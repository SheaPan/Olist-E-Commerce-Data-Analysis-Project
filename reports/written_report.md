## Executive Summary – The Story of Olist

Olist is a fast-growing e-commerce platform in Brazil, connecting thousands of sellers with hundreds of thousands of customers. From **99.44K orders** and **R$16.01M revenue**, the data tells a story about **where growth comes from, who the customers are, and how operations affect satisfaction**.

The narrative emerges across four key pillars:

1. **Sales:** Where revenue comes from and seasonal trends.  
2. **Products:** Which items drive growth and customer value.  
3. **Customers:** Who is satisfied, at-risk, or driving revenue risk.  
4. **Delivery:** Operational efficiency and its direct effect on satisfaction.

By following this story, we can identify **high-impact interventions**, **prevent revenue loss**, and **optimize operations**.

---

## 1. Sales Performance – Growth, Geography, and Peaks

### Metrics Overview
- **Total Revenue:** R$16.01M  
- **Total Orders:** 99,440  
- **Total Sellers:** 3,094  
- **Top States:** São Paulo (R$1.24M), Paraná, Minas Gerais, Rio de Janeiro, Santa Catarina  

**Narrative Insight:** Revenue is concentrated in Southern and Southeastern Brazil, especially São Paulo, which is both a **market leader** and a **risk concentration point**: any issue here affects thousands of customers.

### Seasonal Trends
- Peak month: **November 2017** – 7,544 orders, R$1.19M revenue.  
- Average order value: **R$160–R$167**, stable even during peak months.

**Executive Insight:** Marketing and inventory planning around **peak months** can capture incremental revenue without overspending.

### Yearly Growth
| Year | Revenue | YoY Growth |
|------|---------|------------|
| 2016 | R$59K | – |
| 2017 | R$7.25M | 121% |
| 2018 | R$8.70M | 0.2% |

**Interpretation:** Explosive growth in 2017 stabilized in 2018. The story: **growth has matured**, and sustaining it requires smarter market expansion, not just volume increase.

## 3. Customer Analysis – Who They Are and Why They Matter

### Customer Segments
| Segment | % of Customers | Description |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| Enthusiastic Advocates | – | Highly satisfied, potential promoters |
| Positive Patrons | – | Generally happy, loyalty can be strengthened |
| At-Risk Customers | 15.62% | Quietly dissatisfied, likely to leave |
| High-Risk Detractors | 6.65% | Actively unhappy, urgent attention required |

**Insight:** About **22% of customers are at risk**. Ji Paraná’s high-value accounts alone **represent 72% of revenue at risk**, a small number of customers with outsized impact.

### Satisfaction Trend
- Improved in 2017, stabilized, then declined at the end of 2018.  
- **Trend shows risk accumulating silently** if issues are not addressed.

**Actionable Story:** Intervene early with at-risk customers to **protect revenue and reputation**, especially in high-value accounts.

## 4. Delivery Analysis – Operations Affect Customer Experience

### Delivery Performance
| Delivery Type | % |
|---------------|---|
| Early | 91.96% |
| Late | 6.58% |
| On Time | 1.46% |

### Delivery Times by State
- **Fastest:** São Paulo (SP) – 9 days  
- **Slowest:** Boa Vista (RR) – 92 days  
- **Average:** 24.9 days

**Insight:** Timely delivery is a **critical factor for review scores**:
- Longer delivery → lower satisfaction.  
- High-value items tolerate slightly longer delivery without negative impact.  
- Low-value late deliveries → sharp drop in reviews.

## 5. Strategic Insights – Connecting the Dots

1. **Revenue is concentrated** in São Paulo and peak months. Focused campaigns here maximize impact.  
2. **Products drive growth** – watches, health, and electronics dominate. Bundling and cross-selling increase value.  
3. **Customer risk is real** – ~22% are at risk, especially high-value accounts in small markets.  
4. **Delivery drives satisfaction** – operational inefficiencies directly affect reviews and revenue.

**Executive Takeaway:** Success is not just **sales**, but **coordinated excellence** across product selection, customer engagement, and delivery operations.

---

## 6. Recommendations – Executive Action Plan

1. **Geographic Expansion:** Recruit sellers in Northern/Northeastern states to balance revenue.  
2. **Peak Season Optimization:** Align promotions, inventory, and logistics with seasonal spikes.  
3. **Customer Retention:** Target high-risk and high-value accounts with proactive support.  
4. **Delivery Optimization:** Resolve bottlenecks, prioritize low-value shipments, monitor thresholds for high-value orders.  
5. **Product & Payment Strategy:** Promote top categories, bundle products, diversify payment methods.  
6. **Continuous Monitoring:** Track satisfaction trends, delivery performance, and revenue risks regularly.

**Conclusion – The Story Told by Data**

Olist’s growth is clear, but the story highlights **where revenue and satisfaction are vulnerable**:

- Concentrated sales and revenue risk  
- Customer dissatisfaction pockets  
- Delivery inefficiencies in select regions  
- Untapped product and payment opportunities  

**The path forward:** Data-driven actions across **sales, product, customer, and delivery** ensure **sustainable growth, happy customers, and operational excellence**.
